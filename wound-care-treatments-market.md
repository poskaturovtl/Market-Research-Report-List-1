<p><ul><li>Chronic Wounds</li><li>Acute Wounds</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wound care treatments market is categorized into two primary types: chronic wounds and acute wounds. Chronic wounds are long-lasting, often resistant to healing, commonly seen in conditions like diabetic ulcers and pressure sores. They require specialized treatments such as advanced dressings and bioengineered products. Acute wounds, on the other hand, are typically the result of injuries or surgical procedures and generally heal more quickly. Treatment for acute wounds includes conventional dressings and sutures, focusing on rapid recovery and infection prevention.</p></p>
