<p><p>The food texturizers market includes various types that enhance texture and mouthfeel. Cellulose derivatives offer thickening and stabilizing properties, while gums provide viscosity and emulsification. Pectins are used for gelling, especially in fruit products. Gelatins contribute to a smooth texture, and algae extracts are plant-based alternatives for gelling. Milk proteins improve texture and nutritional value. Starches act as thickeners, inulin adds creaminess and fiber, and dextrins serve as soluble fibers. Carboxymethyl cellulose (CMC) also offers thickening and stabilizing functions.</p></p>
