<p><p>The dental X-Ray equipment market is primarily divided into two types: digital X-Ray systems and analog X-Ray systems. Digital X-Ray systems use electronic sensors to capture and process images, providing higher resolution, quicker results, and reduced radiation exposure for patients. Conversely, analog X-Ray systems rely on film-based technology, requiring chemical processing to develop images. While analog systems are generally less expensive, digital systems are increasingly preferred for their efficiency, image quality, and integration with modern dental practices.</p></p>
