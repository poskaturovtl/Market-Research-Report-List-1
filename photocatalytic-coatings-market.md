<p><p>Photocatalytic coatings are advanced materials that utilize photocatalysis to enhance surface properties, making them effective for self-cleaning, air and water purification, and antimicrobial applications. These coatings typically contain titanium dioxide, which, when exposed to UV light, generates reactive radicals that break down organic pollutants and inhibit the growth of bacteria and mold. </p><p>The Photocatalytic Coatings Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.3% during the forecast period, driven by increasing awareness of environmental sustainability, urbanization, and the need for improved indoor air quality. Industries such as construction, automotive, and textiles are increasingly adopting these coatings to enhance product longevity and functionality. </p><p>Recent trends include the development of non-toxic, visible-light-activated photocatalysts, which broaden their applicability beyond UV-sensitive environments. Additionally, innovative applications in energy-efficient buildings and smart surfaces are gaining traction. Furthermore, the integration of nanotechnology in the formulation of photocatalytic coatings is enhancing their efficiency and effectiveness. As environmental regulations tighten, the demand for photocatalytic solutions that provide dual benefits of aesthetic and functional value is poised for further growth, reflecting a shift towards greener technologies across various sectors.</p></p>
