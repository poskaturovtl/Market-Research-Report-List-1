<p><p>Cellulase (CAS 9012-54-8) is an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of cellulose into glucose. The cellulase market can be segmented into three main types: Endoglucanases (EG), which cleave internal bonds in cellulose chains; Cellobiohydrolases (CBH), which release cellobiose from the ends of the chains; and Beta-glucosidases (BG), which hydrolyze cellobiose into glucose. Each type plays a crucial role in various applications, including biofuel production, animal feed processing, and textile manufacturing, enhancing the efficiency of cellulose degradation.</p></p>
